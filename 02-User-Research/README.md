## 🎯 Research Objective

To understand why customers drop off during digital onboarding and identify friction points in the KYC verification journey while ensuring FCA and AML compliance requirements are maintained.
## 👥 Target Users

### 1. Retail Banking Customers (18–55)
- Opening first current account
- Expect fast, mobile-first experience
- Low tolerance for friction

### 2. SME Customers
- Require business account onboarding
- Higher documentation complexity
- Sensitive to delays

### 3. Internal Stakeholders
- Compliance Officers
- Risk Analysts
- Customer Support Teams
  
 ## 🔍 Research Methods
- Funnel analytics review (drop-off analysis)
- Customer journey mapping
- 10 structured customer interviews
- Internal stakeholder workshops
- Complaint & support ticket analysis
- Benchmarking competitor onboarding flows

 ## 📊 Key Findings
- 38% drop-off occurs at ID verification stage
- Customers struggle with document upload (mobile UX issue)
- Manual KYC review causes 24–48 hour delay
- Confusion around required documentation
- SME onboarding takes 2.5x longer than retail onboarding
- Compliance team overloaded with low-risk cases

 ## 🗺 Customer Journey Stages
1. Account Selection
2. Personal Information Input
3. Document Upload
4. Identity Verification
5. Risk Assessment
6. Account Activation

 ## 📈 Data Insights

- 38% onboarding drop-off rate
- 60% of manual KYC cases classified as low-risk
- 25% of customers abandon during mobile upload
- Average onboarding completion time: 14 minutes

 ## 🔎 Research Conclusion

The primary friction in onboarding is not regulatory complexity but inefficient execution of KYC workflows. 
Opportunity exists to:
- Automate low-risk identity verification
- Simplify mobile UX
- Provide real-time status updates
- Reduce manual review dependency
